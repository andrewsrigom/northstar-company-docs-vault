# Company Handbook Overview

## Purpose

This page is the lightweight entry point for Northstar Company internal policies. It is not the full handbook. Employees should use it to find the right owner when a question spans multiple teams.

## Company Snapshot

Northstar Company is a fictional mid-size B2B software company with remote-first teams and small office hubs. The company uses a shared documentation vault, team Slack channels, ticket queues, and quarterly operating reviews.

## How Policies Are Organized

- People & HR owns employment, leave, onboarding, performance, benefits, and manager practices.
- IT & Security owns devices, access, MFA, data classification, security incidents, and employee software usage.
- Finance owns spend approvals, reimbursements, invoices, budgets, and audit documentation.
- Legal & Compliance owns contracts, privacy review, retention, external commitments, and regulated workflows.
- Operations owns procurement intake, vendor routing, facilities requests, continuity planning, and cross-functional handoffs.
- Product, Engineering, Sales, Customer Success, Marketing, Support, and Data Analytics own their team operating procedures.

## Employee Defaults

Employees should document decisions in the system where the work happens, then link the final decision back to the relevant policy page. If a decision changes an employee obligation, customer commitment, security posture, or spend approval path, the policy owner must review it before rollout.

## When You Cannot Find an Answer

If a policy is missing or unclear, open an Internal Support case with the original question, attempted sources, and the team affected. Support routes the case to the likely policy owner and may mark it as a documentation gap.

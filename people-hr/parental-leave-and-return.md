# Parental Leave and Return to Work

## Eligibility

Full-time employees who work at least 30 hours per week are eligible for Northstar Company parental leave after 90 days of employment. The policy applies to birth, adoption, foster placement, and surrogacy-related parental responsibilities.

## Leave Duration

Primary caregivers may take up to 12 weeks of paid parental leave. Secondary caregivers may take up to 4 weeks of paid parental leave. Leave can be taken continuously or split into two blocks during the first 12 months after the qualifying event.

Employees should submit the request in WorkTrack at least 30 days before the expected leave start date when practical. If the timing is uncertain, the employee should notify their manager and People Operations as soon as possible.

## Return Plan

Before leave starts, the manager and employee should create a return plan that includes:

- expected return date;
- coverage owner for active projects;
- meetings or responsibilities paused during leave;
- systems access that should stay active;
- any part-time ramp schedule for the first two weeks back.

People Operations reviews the plan for policy consistency. Managers own coverage and project handoffs.

## Benefits and Payroll

Health benefits continue during paid parental leave. Payroll continues on the regular pay schedule. Employees should contact People Operations before changing benefit elections due to birth, adoption, or other qualifying life events.

## Escalation

Questions about eligibility, documentation, or pay should go to the People Operations inbox. Questions about project coverage should go to the employee's manager. If a manager and employee disagree on the return plan, People Operations facilitates the decision but does not approve business coverage exceptions without the department leader.

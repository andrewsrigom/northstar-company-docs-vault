# Performance Review Cycle

Owner: People Operations
Last reviewed: 2026-01-30

## Purpose

This page explains the standard performance review cycle for full-time employees.

## Review Timeline

- Week 1: People Operations opens self-reflections and manager review forms.
- Week 2: managers draft feedback and gather peer input.
- Week 3: calibration meetings happen by department.
- Week 4: managers hold review conversations and submit final outcomes.

## Required Inputs

Managers should consider role expectations, business impact, collaboration, customer impact, quality of work, and evidence from the review period. Feedback should be specific and connected to examples.

## Calibration

Department leads host calibration to reduce inconsistency across teams. People Operations facilitates the process but does not override manager observations unless the process is incomplete or inconsistent with policy.

## Out-of-Cycle Reviews

Managers may request an out-of-cycle review for role changes, extended leave return, performance concerns, or promotion readiness. Compensation changes are not guaranteed outside the annual compensation cycle.

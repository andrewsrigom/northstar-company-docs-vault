# Manager One-on-One Guide

Owner: People Operations
Last reviewed: 2026-03-08

## Purpose

This guide helps managers run useful recurring one-on-ones without turning them into status meetings.

## Suggested Agenda

- employee priorities and blockers;
- decisions needed from the manager;
- feedback in both directions;
- growth goals;
- workload and wellbeing signals;
- upcoming absences or coverage needs.

## Documentation

Managers should keep lightweight private notes, but sensitive employee matters should be documented in the approved People Operations system when follow-up is required. Do not store medical details, protected information, or legal concerns in shared team notes.

## Escalation

Escalate to People Operations when a one-on-one raises repeated workload risk, interpersonal conflict, accommodation requests, harassment concerns, payroll questions, or performance concerns that may require formal action.

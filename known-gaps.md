# Known Demo Documentation Gaps

This file intentionally lists gaps that a knowledge assistant should surface during validation.

## Remote Work Policy

The vault now includes remote work guidelines, but it still does not define a full country-by-country approval model, payroll jurisdiction checklist, or accommodation workflow.

## Travel Policy

Finance includes travel expense guidelines, but there is no full travel booking standard with preferred booking tools, airline rules, per diem tables, or international travel safety guidance.

## Department Ownership Matrix

Operations includes a lightweight process ownership matrix, but there is no complete RACI that maps every process to owner, approver, reviewer, backup owner, and escalation path.

## AI Tool Usage

The vault includes draft AI tool usage guidance and working group notes, but it does not define the final approved tool list, retention settings, audit logging expectations, or exception process.

## Employee Onboarding 30/60/90

People & HR has a first-week guide, but there is no company-wide 30/60/90 onboarding plan, contractor onboarding variant, or role-specific milestone template. The first-week page references a handoff diagram, but the process still depends on manager-owned notes.

## Compensation Bands

The compensation cycle FAQ explains process ownership, but the vault intentionally does not include role bands, market ranges, leveling rubrics, or promotion packet examples.

## Customer Data Deletion

Privacy review and retention pages mention data handling, but there is no full customer data deletion runbook with system owners, timelines, and verification evidence.

## Production Access Reviews

IT Security and Engineering mention access requirements, but there is no detailed quarterly production access review procedure with evidence collection and removal workflow.

## Incident Communications

Security and customer escalation documents define ownership, but there is no single approved customer incident communication template for outage, security, and billing scenarios.

## Customer Escalation Severity

Support defines Severity 1 for executive-visible customer escalations, but does not define severity when a renewal-risk customer escalation has no executive visibility. Customer Success may expect faster handling than the Support SLA page promises.

## Partner Program

Sales and Marketing mention partner activity only lightly. There is no complete partner onboarding, referral tracking, co-marketing, or partner support policy.

## Office Emergency Procedures

Operations includes continuity and visitor guidance, but there is no detailed office emergency procedure for fire, weather, medical events, or building access failures.

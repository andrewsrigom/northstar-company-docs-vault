# Data Processing Addendum FAQ

Owner: Legal & Compliance
Last reviewed: 2026-01-24

## What Is A DPA?

A data processing addendum, or DPA, defines data protection obligations when one party processes personal data for another party.

## When Do We Need One?

Northstar may need a DPA when a vendor processes customer, prospect, employee, or contractor personal data. Customers may also ask Northstar to sign their DPA during procurement.

## Who Can Sign?

Only approved signers may sign DPAs. Employees should not click through legal terms, upload signed documents, or accept privacy terms without Legal review.

## What Slows Review?

Review is often delayed when the requester does not know what data is involved, whether subprocessors are used, where data is hosted, or whether the vendor supports deletion requirements.

## Where Is It Stored?

Executed DPAs should be stored with the related contract or vendor record. Do not store signed DPAs only in email threads.

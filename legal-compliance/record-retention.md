# Record Retention

Owner: Legal & Compliance
Last reviewed: 2026-02-19

## Purpose

This page gives a simplified overview of record retention expectations for common business records.

## General Rules

Employees should store business records in approved company systems, not personal drives or local folders. Do not delete records related to active disputes, investigations, audits, customer commitments, or legal holds.

## Common Retention Examples

- executed customer contracts: retain in the contract repository;
- vendor contracts and order forms: retain in the vendor folder;
- approved policies: retain in the documentation vault;
- employee records: retain in the People Operations system;
- security incidents: retain in the incident system with Legal access when needed.

## Legal Hold

When Legal issues a legal hold, normal deletion rules are suspended for the affected records. Employees must follow the hold instructions until Legal releases the hold.

## Questions

If a record type is missing from this page, ask Legal before deleting or moving the record.

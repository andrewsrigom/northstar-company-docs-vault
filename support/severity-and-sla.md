# Support Severity And SLA

Owner: Support
Last reviewed: 2026-03-09

## Purpose

This page defines internal support severity levels for employee-facing requests.

## Severity Levels

Severity 1 means a broad outage, security concern, payroll blocker, customer escalation with executive visibility, or business-critical system failure. First response target is 30 minutes during business hours.

Severity 2 means a department workflow is blocked, a launch is at risk, or several employees are affected. First response target is four business hours.

Severity 3 means a standard request, question, or single-user issue. First response target is two business days.

## Customer Impact Ambiguity

This page says customer escalations with executive visibility are Severity 1, but it does not say how to classify a customer escalation without executive visibility. Some Support leads treat those cases as Severity 2, while Customer Success sometimes asks for Severity 1 handling when a renewal is at risk.

Until this is clarified, Support should document the customer impact, renewal timing, security risk, and affected account tier before assigning severity.

## Ownership

Support triages and routes. Functional teams own policy decisions. IT Security owns security incidents. Finance owns payment decisions. People Operations owns employee policy decisions.

## SLA Exceptions

SLA targets may pause when Support is waiting for requester information, approver decision, vendor response, or policy owner clarification.

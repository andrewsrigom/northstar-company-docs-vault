# Policy Question Triage

## Purpose

This playbook helps Internal Support route employee questions when the answer depends on HR, Finance, IT Security, Legal, or Operations policy.

## First Response

Support should answer directly only when the question maps to a documented policy or runbook. The response should cite the source document and include the next action, owner, and deadline when available.

If the answer is missing, conflicting, or depends on an exception, Support should not guess. The case should be marked as a documentation gap and routed to the policy owner.

## Routing Matrix

- People & HR owns benefits, leave, onboarding, PTO, timekeeping, and employee status questions.
- IT & Security owns access, MFA, devices, incident response, security exceptions, and system permissions.
- Finance owns reimbursement, invoices, payment terms, budget changes, and spend approval.
- Legal & Compliance owns contracts, privacy obligations, retention, external commitments, and regulatory questions.
- Operations owns vendor intake, office requests, procurement handoffs, and cross-functional coordination.

## Escalation Rules

Support escalates to Level 2 when:

- two or more policies appear to conflict;
- an employee asks for an exception;
- the source document is missing a clear owner;
- the case has customer, employee data, legal, payroll, or security impact;
- the same undocumented question appears three times in a month.

## Documentation Gap Workflow

When Support marks a case as a documentation gap, the case note should include the original question, attempted sources, affected team, and suggested owner. The owner should either update the source document or approve a reviewed answer within five business days.
